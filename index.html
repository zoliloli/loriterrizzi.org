GOALOOP – The Goal Market®, Connecting the World Through Goals®

Patent pending digital civic infrastructure for liberty, justice, and market power for all.

With a background in art and art history, an MFA in Film, and having lived in Florence, Italy, Renaissance architects were at the forefront of my mind as I designed Goaloop; both architecture and the Internet are mathematical landscapes. Designing blueprints with musical harmonic principles for more harmonious structures, they believed mathematical harmony reflected a divine order that produced not only aural, but also aesthetic and spatial beauty, creating forms that align with moral excellence and, in the hands of Leonardo da Vinci, physical well-being. I sought to apply a similar philosophy when designing Goaloop (in beta at Goaloop.com).

Toni Morrison's Rose

Essay (unpublished)

Goaloop grew from Toni Morrison's rose to bloom the entire world. This visual progression, her long-stemmed, single crimson rose, taking root as if a seed, was with me as I taught myself to code Goaloop's demo site, hand-drawing every image to reflect human creativity over machine. This process met the influence of Renaissance artists, turning Goaloop's structure into a flower that blooms as a fractal that infinitely blooms others. This mirrors Ms. Morrison's response when an admirer handed her this rose: to give it to another special someone, rippling appreciation and generosity. Goaloop cultivates fertile digital soil to help everyone grow into who they were born to be, regardless of parentage or zip code.

Dear Dr. King, We Have Built a New Edifice

Memoir, including the making of Goaloop (unpublished). Excerpt:

"Dear Dr. King,

We are still struggling to grow a system of peace and equality from the violent and unequal system at our roots. The economic movement you sparked in the days before your death is unfinished. How much longer will it take to reform our economic system? How many more lives and lifetimes and libraries will this take?

What if we were to start all over again, knowing what we know now? What if we could create a brand new economic system that would work within the current one, accepting of the status quo, and, as it is adopted, would grow us out of our unequal system and into one created for growth by all from the start? If love has never been a popular movement, our new system could teach love like our one now tempts hate.

I'm writing to you in Riverside Church, a short walk from where I now live. When I entered, they were tuning the organs for Easter service. I listened to your speech again from April 4, 1967 while seated in the second pew. I went downstairs to look for a tabletop to write this letter, finding only dark hallways and a bright basketball court.

Someone directed me to a location upstairs, and when I reached it, I couldn't believe my eyes: there is a large photograph of you under an arch. I'm looking up at you as I write this letter. I was born days after your assassination, on Easter Sunday. We've transcribed your words into code. We have built a new edifice."

I Want My Privacy and Your Transparency

Essay (unpublished)

How do we reconcile that we often want privacy for ourselves yet transparency from others online? Goaloop's system resolves this paradox. Excerpt:

"As we become more connected electronically, we know each other less, isolated in worlds of agreement as algorithms draw smaller islands around each of us. Seeking agreement is a form of comfort, an understandable temptation in an unjust world.

But these algorithms have been written to serve private profits, not us. Their collateral damage has been a stripping of our emotional investment in each other, of our exploration of each other. The result is a teaching of our emotional divestment in the other.

Losing control of our private data, trust, and the roots and joys that trust brings, we are unmooring ourselves from each other. As we drift apart, our need for belonging is still anchored within us, making us susceptible to open arms that claim us, including violent ones. It's sometimes hard to see a revolution underway other than the quiet gutting of our inner lives in exchange for outer ones, shells easily filled by the strongest predator, while we're swept away by content streams throughout the day."

Lori Terrizzi

Lori Terrizzi is the CEO/Founder and Site Architect of GOALOOP – The Goal Market®, Connecting the World through Goals®. Terrizzi holds an MFA from Columbia University in Film and is a Phi Beta Kappa graduate of Rutgers College, where she was awarded the Vanderbilt Scholarship for the Study of Art & Music. Goaloop is the product of her life-long focus on freedom, issues of identity, and study of the relationship between words and images, form and content, equity and ability, truth and memory, power and substance, one of us and all of us.

Founding client of Yale Law School's Entrepreneurship & Innovation Clinic, Goaloop is patent pending and answers a fundamental question: Will today's interconnectivity become a web of surveillance that traps us as prey, or a trellis that supports growth toward fulfillment? Goaloop ensures the latter: a personal trellis for each of us, connected to build a better world for everyone. Goaloop helps you reach your goals and connects the world through goals.

Goaloop is an outgrowth of Terrizzi's prior position as Executive Director of the non-profit organization of writers aiding writers, founded by Board Member Salman Rushdie, continuing the work of the International Parliament of Writers in the United States. Principally working with the organization's VP, Board Member Caryl Phillips, the Board of Directors also included President Russell Banks, Carolyn Forché, Jayne Cortez, Derek Walcott, Michael Ondaatje, Dionne Brand, and Wole Soyinka, with Advisory Board Members Toni Morrison and Margaret Atwood.

While protecting the creative freedom of artists writing in dozens of languages, the Internet was becoming a new 'language.' "Scaling" entered the lexicon. What were we scaling? Would it turn everyone into a 'persecuted writer?' How could we rewrite the semiotics of the Internet to help everyone achieve freedom?

These questions dovetailed with the collapse of Lehman Brothers, where Terrizzi had previously worked, provoking her imagination to conceive a new, accessible, transparent market. While at Lehman, she worked on a project with Ernest Green of the Little Rock Nine, whose transition from the non-profit sector to Wall Street made a lasting impression. She then produced for American Movie Classics on a weekly television show awarded the Emmy® for Best Series in its category for two consecutive years, before founding a non-profit film platform on the eve of YouTube, mentored by Carnegie Corporation president Vartan Gregorian. Terrizzi channeled these experiences to design Goaloop – The Goal Market®, a new cross-sector marketplace where the only cost of entry is a goal – and where every goal becomes a Govie™, an automatic 'goal movie.'

As VP of the new James Baldwin Society at Rutgers, Terrizzi began writing Mr. Baldwin a letter. He had agreed to attend the inaugural meeting; he died two months before. The letter remained unfinished – until Terrizzi completed it in a forthcoming video that tells Goaloop's origin story. The James Baldwin Estate granted Terrizzi permission to include an interview of Mr. Baldwin in this video, "Goaloop – Street of Dreams," which includes two screens:

The story Raising Liberty: The Construction of the Statue of Liberty as Reconstruction Was Under Attack served as a metaphor for the construction of Goaloop; it is being made into a movie.

The granddaughter of Armenian Genocide survivors and Sicilian immigrants, and the daughter of first-generation professors, Terrizzi was raised in Amherst, Massachusetts, where she formed a mission at an early age to build upon the freedoms she was born into: Artistic freedom for all. Believing "no one is free until we all are free" makes equality a prerequisite for freedom. Artistic freedom is elusive without economic freedom.

A formative experience included moving to Florence, Italy for a semester in high school, where Renaissance men were known, but not one mention of a Renaissance woman; where art is not relegated to museums, it is woven into life; where high school exams were verbal "interrogations" in front of the class in Italian, and the curriculum included Design. Terrizzi later bypassed scholarship opportunities to study architecture at RISD and biomedical engineering at Rutgers, called instead to study history through art, and human character through film.

Insisting on turning the privilege of becoming who you truly are into a right, and make it within everyone's reach, Goaloop fulfills Terrizzi's personal mission to build a system that supports our nature, utilizing technology for growth and innovation, instead of becoming more like the machines we create. Goaloop integrates AI while advancing Human Intelligence.

At the center of Goaloop's origin story is Toni Morrison's rose, still atop a bookshelf in Manhattan, where Terrizzi resides.
